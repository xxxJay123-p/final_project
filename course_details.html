In a land where dreams and play entwine,
A boy with wonder, pure and fine.
His toy deer, crafted with care,
A world of joy they both would share.

Imagination danced in their play,
Underneath the sun's warm ray.
The boy, with laughter, filled the air,
As his toy deer became a friend so rare.

With every bite, a tale was spun,
Of adventures wild, under the golden sun.
The toy transformed with each sweet chew,
A magical journey only they knew.

Through fields of green and skies so blue,
The boy and toy, a bond that grew.
In a world of make-believe, so sincere,
The boy found joy in his toy deer.

No harm, no pain, just playful delight,
As day turned to dusk and then to night.
The boy and his toy, an everlasting pair,
In a world of dreams, beyond compare.
